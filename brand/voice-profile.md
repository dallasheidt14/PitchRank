# PitchRank Voice Profile

## Voice Summary

PitchRank sounds like the parent two years ahead of you who's already figured out the system — knowledgeable but never condescending, direct but never cold. We speak to parents who check rankings at 11pm, who drive 3 hours for a showcase, who need one clear answer: is my kid on the right team? The voice respects their investment (financial and emotional) while cutting through the noise with data they can actually trust.

## Core Personality Traits

- **Expert but accessible:** Deep on the algorithm and the landscape; never hides behind jargon. Explains "why" when it helps. Parents spending $5K-$15K/year deserve straight answers, not black boxes.
- **Direct:** Gets to the point. Short sentences. No corporate filler. These parents are busy — practices Tuesday/Thursday, games Saturday, tournaments every other weekend. Respect their time.
- **Trustworthy:** Backs claims with data and method. Comfortable saying what we don't know or what's uncertain. In a world where GotSoccer rankings reward tournament quantity over quality, trust is our moat.
- **Warm without being soft:** Supportive of parents' investment and pride in their kids. Never talks down. Never judges the parent who checks rankings 200 times a year — we built this for them.
- **Validating without pandering:** Parents want to know their decisions are right. We give them honest data, not false comfort. If their team is #47, we show why #47 is exactly right — or what it would take to move up.

## Tone Spectrum

| Dimension | Position | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Formal ↔ Casual | Casual, but not sloppy | Contractions yes; slang sparingly. Professional clarity. Parent-to-parent, not brand-to-consumer. |
| Serious ↔ Playful | Mostly serious, occasional lightness | Soccer is serious to this audience — $10K/year serious. Wit only when it fits. Never joke about rankings or placement. |
| Reserved ↔ Bold | Confident, not arrogant | Clear claims ("rankings you can trust"). Bold on data. Humble on what data can't tell you (development, happiness, college outcomes). |
| Simple ↔ Sophisticated | Simple words, sophisticated ideas | PowerScore, v53e, ML — explain in plain language. Parents are smart (college-educated, $100K+ HH) but not data scientists. |
| Warm ↔ Direct | Direct but not cold | "You" focused. Empathy for the anxiety (tryouts, club switching, pathway confusion); clarity in the solution. |

## Vocabulary

**Words/phrases to USE:**
- rankings, stand, where you stand, where your team stands — core product language
- team, club, age group, tier, division — precise domain terms
- parents, coaches, club directors — audience identity
- transparent, fair, data-driven — trust signals
- see how you stack up, know where you stand — transformation language
- PowerScore, algorithm, 13-layer, weekly update — mechanism language
- common opponents, strength of schedule — language parents already use
- pathway, ECNL, MLS NEXT, GA, NPL — league vocabulary they know
- flight, showcase, ID camp — tournament vocabulary
- real game data, verified results — differentiation from GotSoccer

**Words/phrases to AVOID:**
- comprehensive, best-in-class, cutting-edge — generic marketing
- leverage, synergy, ecosystem — corporate jargon
- game-changing, revolutionary — hype (parents are skeptical of hype)
- obviously, it goes without saying — filler
- elite (overused in youth soccer to the point of meaninglessness)
- "just" (minimizes: "just check the rankings")
- guaranteed, proven (ranking data is probabilistic, not guaranteed)
- disrupting, innovating — Silicon Valley speak alienates soccer parents
- comprehensive solution — sounds like SaaS, not like a tool for parents

**Domain terms to use naturally (no explanation needed):**
- U10, U12, U14, U16, U19 (age groups)
- ECNL, MLS NEXT, GA, NPL, ECNL-RL (leagues)
- D1, D2, D3, NAIA (college divisions)
- DOC (Director of Coaching)
- Showcase, ID camp, tryouts

**Domain terms to explain on first use (blog/SEO):**
- PowerScore (our ranking metric)
- v53e (our algorithm version)
- Merge resolution (how we handle duplicate teams)
- Strength of schedule (what it means in our system)

**Jargon level:** Moderate — match the audience's existing vocabulary. Parents know ECNL and U14. They don't know "regression model" or "Bayesian inference." Translate technical concepts.

**Profanity:** Never

## Rhythm & Structure

**Sentences:** Mix of short (5-10 words) and medium (12-18). Rarely long. One idea per sentence when explaining something technical. Short sentences for emphasis after data points.

**Paragraphs:** Short. 1-3 sentences max. White space. Scannable. Parents are reading on phones between practices.

**Openings:** Often a direct benefit, a clear question, or a stat that stops scrolling. Avoid "In this post we'll explore..." Start with what parents care about: their team, their ranking, their decision.

**Formatting:** Headers, bullets, bold for key terms. Minimal emoji. Numbers and specifics over vagueness. Tables for comparisons (parents love comparing). Rankings and scores should be prominent.

## POV & Address

**First person:** We (brand); I only in founder story or personal blog.
**Reader address:** You. Direct. "Your team." "Your rankings." "You can see."
**Relationship stance:** Guide / expert peer. The parent who's been through club switching, tryout anxiety, and tournament weekends — and also happens to have built the algorithm. Not a guru; not a distant SaaS brand.

## Emotional Intelligence Guide

**What our audience feels (and how to respond):**

| Emotion | They're Feeling | Our Voice Does |
|---------|----------------|----------------|
| Pride | "My kid's team is #12" | Validate. Give them shareable proof. Make it easy to brag. |
| Anxiety | "Should we switch clubs?" | Provide data, not opinions. Show the comparison. Let the numbers speak. |
| Frustration | "These rankings are rigged" | Acknowledge past experiences with bad systems. Show our method. Transparency is the answer. |
| FOMO | "Everyone's going to ECNL" | Give context. Show what the data says about league quality at their age group. |
| Sunk cost | "We've spent $50K already" | Never question their investment. Help them see what the data says about their current path. |
| Skepticism | "Why should I pay for rankings?" | Lead with what's free. Show what Premium reveals that free doesn't. Never push. |

**What we NEVER do:**
- Exploit anxiety to sell upgrades
- Make parents feel bad about their choices
- Promise college outcomes based on rankings
- Judge parents for caring about rankings (they should care)
- Use guilt language ("you're missing out")
- Pretend rankings are the only thing that matters

## Platform Adaptations

| Platform | Tone Shift | Structure | Length |
|----------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Email | Warmer, more personal. Parent-to-parent feel. Acknowledge the grind (practices, travel, costs). | Short paragraphs, one CTA. Open with what matters to them right now (rankings update, tryout season, tournament preview). | 150-300 words |
| LinkedIn | Slightly more professional. Speak to coaches and DOCs too. Data-forward. | Single-idea posts, line breaks. Lead with a stat or insight. | 100-200 words |
| Twitter/X | Punchiest. One stat, one take, one ranking fact. Shareable. | One idea, no fluff. Rankings updates, surprising data points, head-to-head previews. | Under 280 chars |
| Blog/SEO | More thorough, still voiced. Explain methodology. Answer "how are teams ranked" and "is my team good." | Headers, lists, examples, tables. FAQ sections for PAA. | 1,500-2,500 words |
| Landing Page | Benefit-focused. Speak to the core question: "Is my kid on the right team?" Social proof from real parents. | Short sentences, CTA-heavy. Rankings preview above fold. Pricing clear and simple. | Varies by section |
| Push/Notification | Ultra-brief. Ranking movement is the hook. | "[Team] moved from #15 to #12 this week" or "New rankings are live." | Under 100 chars |

## Example Phrases

**On-brand (sounds like us):**
- "See where your team stands — rankings built from real game data."
- "We run 25K+ teams through a 13-layer algorithm every week. Here's what that means for yours."
- "Parents and coaches deserve rankings they can trust. Not opinions. Not tournament politics. Data."
- "Your team moved up 3 spots this week. Here's why."
- "ECNL or MLS NEXT? Here's how the data compares at U14."
- "You already spend $5,000+ a year on club soccer. Shouldn't you know where your team actually stands?"

**Off-brand (doesn't sound like us):**
- "Our comprehensive, best-in-class solution leverages cutting-edge ML." — Generic, jargon-heavy. Parents tune out instantly.
- "Obviously, everyone wants to know their ranking." — Filler opening. Start with the value.
- "We're revolutionizing youth soccer." — Hype. Parents have heard this from every new league, app, and platform.
- "Don't miss out on critical ranking data!" — FOMO exploitation. We inform, we don't scare.
- "Subscribe now before it's too late!" — False urgency. Rankings aren't going anywhere.
- "Trust us, our algorithm is the best." — Prove it, don't claim it. Show the method.

## Do's and Don'ts

**DO:**
- Lead with the benefit or the question the reader already has
- Use specific numbers (13 layers, 25K teams, 365-day window) when they build trust
- Mirror the language parents use in forums ("common opponents," "where we stand," "pathway")
- Make rankings shareable — parents want to show them off
- Acknowledge the financial and emotional investment
- Give context for what rankings mean (not just a number)
- Keep CTAs clear and single
- Speak to tryout season, tournament weekends, and club-switching moments

**DON'T:**
- Use marketing buzzwords or corporate speak
- Overpromise or claim we're the only/best without backing it
- Write long paragraphs or dense blocks
- Sound like a committee or a generic SaaS
- Exploit parent anxiety to drive upgrades
- Make promises about college outcomes
- Ignore the secondary audience (coaches, DOCs) — they're 10% but they're influencers
- Rank kids under U10 without context about what that means for development
