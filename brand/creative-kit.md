## Brand Colors

- **Primary:** #0B5345 — Forest Green (athletic, trustworthy, primary brand)
- **Secondary:** #1a6b5c — Lighter Forest (hover states, gradients, secondary UI)
- **Accent:** #F4D03F — Electric Yellow (CTAs, highlights, energy)
- **Background:** #0f1419 — Deep charcoal (dark UI) / #ffffff — White (light UI)
- **Text:** #1a1a1a — Near black (body) / #f8fafc — Near white (on dark)

## Typography Direction

- **Headlines:** Oswald — bold, condensed, athletic. All-caps or title case for impact. High contrast, sporty feel.
- **Body:** DM Sans — clean, readable, neutral weight. Slightly warm, approachable. Use for all body copy and UI.
- **Display/Hero:** Oswald — oversized, condensed, all-caps for hero headlines and key numbers (e.g. rank, PowerScore).

## Visual Style

- **Photography preference:** Real youth soccer when possible — fields, teams, moments. Warm natural light. Avoid stiff stock poses; prefer action or candid energy.
- **Illustration style:** Minimal. If used: flat or subtle gradients, clear shapes, no heavy outlines. Data viz (charts, rankings) should feel clean and legible, not decorative.
- **Overall mood:** Confident, clear, athletic. Trustworthy and energetic. Premium but accessible — not corporate, not childish.
- **What to avoid:** Clip art, neon/fluorescent outside accent, dark/gothic, overly playful or cartoon, generic stock-soccer clichés.

## Competitor Visual References

- **GotSport / league sites:** Clean data tables and rankings; learn legibility and hierarchy. Avoid their often-dated UI feel.
- **Sports apps (e.g. ESPN, The Athletic):** Strong typography and score/stat presentation; aim for that clarity without the heavy commercial look.
- **Youth sports brands:** Avoid overly “kid” aesthetics; PitchRank speaks to parents and coaches, so tone should feel serious and credible.
