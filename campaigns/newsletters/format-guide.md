# PitchRank Weekly — Newsletter Format Guide

## Format: Data Briefing (Morning Brew style, adapted for youth soccer)

**Frequency:** Every Monday after rankings update
**Length:** 300-500 words (parents read on phones between practices)
**Delivery:** Automated via marketing pipeline → Beehiiv API
**Voice:** Expert peer. Direct. Data-forward. No fluff.

---

## Recurring Sections

### 1. Hook (1 sentence)
The single biggest mover or most surprising data point this week.
Stops the scroll. Gets them into the email.
Format: Bold stat + team name + context.

### 2. Top Climbers (table)
Top 5 teams that moved up the most. Table: rank, team, state, change.
Green color for positive movement. Parents scan this first.

### 3. Biggest Drops (table)
Top 5 teams that dropped. Table: rank, team, state, change.
Red color for negative movement. Equally engaging — "is my team here?"

### 4. State Spotlight
Rotating state with the most ranking movement this week.
Top 3 teams from that state. Drives engagement from parents in that state
who will share with their club group chats.

### 5. By the Numbers
3 quick stats: total teams ranked, biggest single jump, states covered.
Reinforces scale and credibility every week.

### 6. CTA
"See Where Your Team Stands" button → pitchrank.io/rankings
Subtext: mechanism proof (25K teams, 13 layers, every Monday)

### 7. Footer
Social links (X, Instagram). Powered by PitchRank tagline.
Beehiiv handles unsubscribe compliance.

---

## Subject Line Formula

**Data-forward (preferred — per learnings):**
"PitchRank Weekly: {team_name} jumped {N} spots | {date}"

Always includes the biggest mover's name and movement.
Team names truncated to 30 chars for mobile readability.

## Send Timing

**Day:** Monday (immediately after rankings update, ~10-11 AM MT)
**Rationale:** Rankings are freshest. Parents checking on Monday.
**Avoid:** Saturday morning (game day), Sunday afternoon (tournament recovery)
