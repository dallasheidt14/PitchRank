## The North Carolina Youth Soccer Landscape

North Carolina isn't one soccer market. It's at least three — and they play very differently.

### Geographic Soccer Regions

- **Charlotte Metro** (Charlotte, Concord, Huntersville, Matthews) — The state's largest soccer market. Home to Charlotte Soccer Academy (376 teams), Charlotte Independence SC, Queen City Mutiny FC, and Charlotte Eagles. Dense competition, strong club infrastructure, and year-round outdoor play.
- **Triangle** (Raleigh, Durham, Chapel Hill, Cary) — Anchored by NCFC (327 teams), Wake FC, and Triangle United. College-town culture drives coaching quality. Home to NC Courage and NCFC's professional pipeline.
- **Triad / Central NC** (Greensboro, Winston-Salem, High Point) — NC Fusion leads this region with 177 teams. Growing competitive depth with strong regional tournaments.
- **Coastal NC** (Wilmington, Jacksonville, Outer Banks) — Wilmington Hammerheads FC (97 teams) is the dominant program. Fewer clubs but increasingly competitive, with players often traveling to Charlotte or the Triangle for higher-level play.
- **Piedmont / Foothills** (Fayetteville, Asheville, Hickory) — Highland FC, Carolina Velocity FC, Carolina Core FC Youth, and Fayetteville SC / Villarreal Force Academy serve this region. Developing scene with strong local loyalty.

## How North Carolina Soccer Rankings Actually Work

Most ranking systems — GotSoccer being the biggest — only count tournament games and reward quantity over quality. A team that enters 15 tournaments and beats weak opponents can outrank a team that plays a tougher schedule but enters fewer events.

That's not how PitchRank works.

### What PitchRank Tracks for North Carolina Teams

We track **every game we can find** — league play, tournaments, friendlies, showcases — across all 2,606 North Carolina teams.

Here's the short version of how your team's ranking is calculated:

1. **Base score** — Wins, losses, draws, and goal differential give us a starting point
2. **Strength of schedule** — Beating a top-ranked Charlotte SA squad means more than beating a developmental team. Losing to a strong opponent hurts less than losing to a weak one.
3. **Recency** — Last month's games count more than games from 10 months ago
4. **Consistency** — Steady performance ranks higher than wild swings between blowout wins and bad losses

The result is a **PowerScore between 0.0 and 1.0**:

- **0.85+** = Elite national-level team
- **0.70-0.84** = Top competitive tier
- **0.50-0.69** = Solid competitive team
- **0.30-0.49** = Developing/mid-level
- **Below 0.30** = Recreational or limited game data

### What Rankings Don't Tell You

Rankings measure competitive results. They can't measure:

- **Individual player development** — A top-ranked team might not be the best fit for your child's growth
- **Coaching quality** — Some lower-ranked teams have better developmental coaches than win-now programs
- **Team culture** — Your kid's enjoyment matters more than a number
- **College fit** — D3 coaches care about GPA and character more than PowerScore

If a club director sells you on rankings alone but can't explain their development philosophy, that's a red flag.

## How to Use North Carolina Rankings When Choosing a Club

Tryout season in North Carolina runs April through June. Rankings are one tool in your decision-making kit — here's how to use them wisely.

### Questions to Ask Club Directors

When you're comparing Charlotte Soccer Academy vs NCFC vs NC Fusion:

1. **"How do your teams' rankings trend over time?"** — Upward trends suggest good coaching. Flat or declining suggests stagnation.
2. **"What's the strength of schedule for this age group?"** — Are they playing real competition or scheduling easy wins?
3. **"How do your North Carolina rankings compare to teams we'd face at regionals?"** — National context matters if your child has college ambitions.
4. **"How many players from this age group have moved to ECNL or MLS NEXT?"** — Player progression matters more than team rank.

### Finding the Right Competition Level

The best team for your child isn't always the highest-ranked one. Look for a team that:

- Plays opponents 10-20 ranking positions above AND below them
- Gives your child 30+ minutes per game
- Challenges without crushing confidence
- Fits your family's travel budget and schedule

**North Carolina's advantage:** With clubs spread across Charlotte, the Triangle, the Triad, and the coast, you have options at every competitive level — and travel between the state's major soccer hubs is manageable within a few hours.

### Red Flags

- **Cherry-picking opponents** — Teams that pad records against weak competition. Their ranking will plateau.
- **Wild ranking swings** — Could signal roster instability or inconsistent coaching
- **Ranking guarantees** — No legitimate club can promise specific rankings

## The Charlotte vs Triangle Rivalry

North Carolina's biggest soccer rivalry isn't on the professional pitch — it's in youth club soccer. Charlotte and the Triangle produce the state's highest-ranked teams across nearly every age group, and the two metro areas regularly meet in state cup play, ECNL matchups, and showcase tournaments.

What this means for parents:

- **Charlotte clubs** tend to have larger rosters and more teams per age group, giving players more options within a single organization
- **Triangle clubs** benefit from proximity to three major universities (UNC, Duke, NC State) and the coaching talent that comes with it
- **Cross-region play** is common and valuable — teams that regularly play opponents from the other metro area tend to develop faster

If your team only plays within your metro, rankings can be misleading. The true test comes when Charlotte meets Raleigh.
